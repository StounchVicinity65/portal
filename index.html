Physics

Historically, physics evolved from natural philosophy and became a modern science during the Scientific Revolution, with major contributions from Newton, Maxwell, and Einstein.

Key Figures: Isaac Newton, Albert Einstein, Galileo Galilei, James Clerk Maxwell

Motion is one of the central concepts in physics.

Chemistry

Modern chemistry developed from alchemy, with key advancements from Lavoisier, Dalton, and Mendeleev.

Key Figures: Dmitri Mendeleev, Marie Curie, Antoine Lavoisier, John Dalton

The periodic table is fundamental to understanding chemical behavior.

Biology

Biology advanced through major discoveries such as evolution by Darwin, genetics by Mendel, and microbiology by Pasteur.

Key Figures: Charles Darwin, Gregor Mendel, Louis Pasteur, Aristotle

Cell theory and evolution are central principles of biology.
